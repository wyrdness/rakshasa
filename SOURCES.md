# Sources & References

## Primary Religious Texts

### Hindu Epics
- Valmiki. *Ramayana* (c. 500-100 BCE). The primary epic featuring Ravana and the Rakshasa kingdom of Lanka.
- Vyasa (traditional attribution). *Mahabharata* (c. 400 BCE - 400 CE). Contains various Rakshasa characters including Ghatotkacha.
- *Rigveda* (c. 1500 BCE). Earliest Vedic text mentioning Rakshasas as demonic beings.

### Buddhist Texts
- *Jataka Tales*. Collection of Buddhist birth stories featuring Yakshas and Rakshasas.
- Various Pali Canon texts discussing conversion of Rakshasas to dharma protectors.

## On Cultural Appropriation

Researchers should be aware that Rakshasas are:
- Part of living religious traditions
- Sacred to millions of Hindus and Buddhists
- Not generic "fantasy monsters"
- Deeply embedded in cultural and spiritual contexts

When studying or referencing Rakshasas:
1. Consult primary religious texts
2. Seek guidance from Hindu/Buddhist scholars
3. Distinguish authentic tradition from Western adaptations
4. Avoid reducing religious figures to entertainment tropes
5. Recognize ongoing cultural significance
